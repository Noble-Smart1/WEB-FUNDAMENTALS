Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) - White Water Rafting


1. What is white water rafting?

White outdoor adventure activity where participants navigate a river's rapids aboard inflatable rafts. It's a thrilling and challenging experience that combines teamwork,skill, and a love for the great outdoors.

2. Is white water rafting safe?

Yes, when conducted by experienced and certified guides with proper safety measures in place, white water rafting is generally considered safe. Our guides are trained professionals, and we adhere to strict safety standards to ensure a secure and enjoyable experience.

3. What do I need to bring for a white water rafting trip?

Participants should bring swimwear, secure water shoes, sunscreen, and a change of clothes. It's advisable to leave valuables, like jewelry and electronic devices, at home or in designated storage provided by the rafting company.

4. Do I need previous rafting experience?

No prior rafting experience is required for most of our trips. Our guides provide thorough safety briefings and instructions before each excursion, ensuring everyone is well-prepared for the adventure.

5. What is the minimum age and weight limit?

The minimum age and weight limits vary depending on the river and specific trip. Generally, participants must be at least 8 years old, and weight limits may apply. Check the details of each trip for specific requirements.

6. Are there different levels of difficulty for white water rafting?

Yes, our trips offer varying levels of difficulty, from beginner-friendly to advanced. Choose a trip that matches your skill level and comfort, and our guides will provide the appropriate guidance.

7. What class of rapids do you offer?

We offer a range of rapids, from Class I (easy, fast-moving water) to Class V (extremely difficult and violent rapids). Each trip is graded based on the International Scale of River Difficulty, allowing you to choose an adventure that suits your preferences.
